Objective Items

  1. What is a computer network? It’s a collection of computing devices that are connected in various ways to communicate and share resources.

  2. To what does the word node (host) refer? Addressable device attached to a network.

  3. Describe the client/server model. A distributed approach in which a client makes requests of a server and the server responds.

  4. What is a P2P network? A decentralized approach that shares resources and responsibilities among many “peer” computers.

  5. Distinguish between the following LAN topologies: ring, star, and bus. How does the shape of the topology influence message flow through a LAN? A ring topology connects all nodes in a closed loop on which messages travel in one direction. The nodes of a ring network pass along messages until they reach their destination. A star topology centers on one node to which all others are connected and through which all messages are sent. In a bus topology, all nodes are connected to a single communication line that carries messages in both directions. The nodes on the bus check any message sent on the bus but ignore any that are not addressed to them.

  6. Messages sent across the Internet are divided into packets. What is a packet, and why are messages divided into them? A packet is a unit of data sent across a network. To improve the efficiency of transferring data over a shared communication line.

  7. Name three technologies for connecting a home computer to the Internet. Phone modem, cable modem, digital subscriber line (DSL).

  8. Explain the term packet switching. The approach to network communication in which packets are individually routed to their destination, then reassembled.

  9. 53. What problems arise due to packet switching? And how can they be solved? A path may be blocked due to a down machine, and a router might send a packet along an alternative route. A communication line may span a long distance, and the signal may weaken. Repeaters are installed periodically along the line to strengthen and propagate the signal.

  10. What does the acronym of MAN represent? Metropolitan-area network.

  11. How is a hostname composed? It consists of the computer name followed by the domain name.

  12. Why was the IPv6 protocol created for IP addresses? The IPv4 strategy is limited in the number of unique computers (around four billion) it could identify. As Internet use increased, especially with the popularity of mobile computing devices such as smartphones and tablets, the supply of unique 4-byte IPv4 addresses dwindled.

  13. What is a top-level domain name (TLD)? The last section of a domain name specifying the type of organization or its country of origin.

  14. An engineer notices that the data received by computers at the two ends of a bus LAN contain many errors. What do you think is the problem? What can be done to solve the problem? An engineer notices that the data received by computers at the two ends of a bus LAN contain many errors. This could indicate issues with the bus itself or interference. Solutions would include checking cable integrity, ensuring proper termination, or replacing damaged sections.

  15. A small part of a bus LAN with 200 stations is damaged. How many other stations are affected by this damage? In a bus topology, when a station sends a frame to another computer, all computers receive the frame and check its destination address. So, no computer is affected by this damage.

  16. A small part of a star LAN with 200 stations is damaged. How many other stations are affected by this damage? If the core node station is among those 200 stations, then all the stations will be affected by this damage. Otherwise, no computer is affected by this damage.
