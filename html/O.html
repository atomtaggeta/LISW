Object
An instance of a class that encapsulates data and behavior in object-oriented programming.
Output
The information produced by a computer program after processing the input.
Operation
A function or action that can be performed on data or tasks in programming.
Overhead
The additional resources required to manage a system or process, not directly related to the primary operations.
OAuth
An open standard for access delegation commonly used for token-based authentication.
Optimization
The process of making a system or design as effective or functional as possible, often in terms of performance.
Operator
A symbol or keyword used to perform operations on variables and values, like +, -, *, and /.
Overwrite
To replace existing data with new data, usually in files or databases.
Objectivity
The quality of being objective, without bias, important in data analysis and programming.
Oscillation
The fluctuation of a signal or system around a central point, often discussed in terms of computational frequency.
ORM
Object-Relational Mapping, a technique for converting data between incompatible type systems in databases.
Online
Refers to systems or services that are connected to the internet or active in real time.
Offline
Refers to systems or services that are not connected to the internet or are not active in real time.
Open Source
Software with source code that anyone can inspect, modify, and enhance.
Overfitting
A modeling error that occurs when a machine learning model learns the training data too well, including noise and outliers.
Openness
The degree to which systems are open to change, collaboration, and sharing of information and resources.
Oscillator
A circuit or device that produces a continuous signal oscillating between two states, often used in signal processing.
Overwrite
The act of replacing old data with new data in a file or database.
