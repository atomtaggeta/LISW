Replication
The process of duplicating data or processes to ensure consistency across systems.
Recursion
A method where the solution to a problem depends on solutions to smaller instances of the same problem.
Router
A device that forwards data packets between computer networks, directing traffic on the internet.
Runtime
The period during which a program is running, as opposed to when it is being compiled.
Repository
A central location where data is stored and managed, often used for version control of code.
Responsive Design
An approach to web design that makes web pages render well on a variety of devices and window or screen sizes.
Regular Expression
A sequence of characters that defines a search pattern, primarily used for string matching.
Redundancy
The inclusion of extra components or systems to increase reliability in case of failure.
Reflection
The ability of a program to examine and modify its own structure and behavior at runtime.
Resource
A source or supply from which benefit is produced, such as CPU, memory, or file storage.
RDBMS
Relational Database Management System, a database management system based on the relational model.
Routing
The process of selecting paths in a network along which to send network traffic.
Role-Based Access Control
An access control method where permissions are assigned to roles, rather than individual users.
Risk Analysis
The process of identifying and assessing potential risks that could negatively affect projects or operations.
Rendering
The process of generating an image from a model by means of computer programs.
